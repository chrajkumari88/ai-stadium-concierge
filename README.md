# AI Stadium Concierge

## Problem Statement

International fans attending FIFA World Cup 2026 matches are often visiting
an unfamiliar stadium, sometimes in a country where they don't speak the
local language. Large stadiums are disorienting — signage is limited to one
or two languages, staff are stretched thin during high-traffic periods, and
finding something specific (an accessible restroom, the nearest metro exit,
a particular gate) usually means physically hunting for a human to ask.

Static maps and signage can't answer a specific question ("where's the
*nearest* accessible restroom to *my* gate") — they show everything, and the
visitor has to do the work of finding the relevant piece themselves. A
generic chatbot without real stadium data would just make up plausible but
wrong directions.

**Solved looks like:** a visitor asks a question in their own language, in
plain conversational text, and gets a short, accurate answer grounded in
real stadium data.

## Tech Stack
React + TypeScript + Vite (frontend), Node/Express (backend proxy), Groq API
(LLM), custom keyword-based retrieval (RAG pattern).
